8. Conclusion

This project successfully established a validated mechanical foundation for a RoboMaster-compliant bipedal wheel-leg robot capable of 200 mm step climbing. Through systematic exploration of tracks, big wheels, and wheel-leg architectures, the five-bar linkage big wheel-leg design proved to offer the best balance of mechanical advantage, compactness, and adaptiveness to future terrain challenges.

Prototype testing confirmed the dynamic motion behaviour of the robot, viability of the cascaded joint, hubless wheel structure, and linkage-driven actuation, providing clear insight into mechanical requirements and tolerance management. These results form a solid basis for the next development stage, which will focus on designing a full assembly, dynamic control, balancing strategies, and software integration.

Overall, the project demonstrates the foundation of a carefully optimised wheel-leg system that could reliably overcome terrain challenges and competition-ready. The design direction chosen provides a strong platform for continued development toward a fully operational RoboMaster step-climbing robot.
