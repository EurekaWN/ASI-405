3. Problem Clarification

To guide the design process, a clear direction is required in the form of a problem clarification statement, supported by measurable criteria outlined in a design statement. Therefore, a competitor analysis is carried out first to determine the appropriate design framework to use.

3.1 Competitor Analysis

The primary competitors in the RoboMaster University Championship (RMUC) are international teams, predominantly from top Chinese universities. Many of these teams have already developed robots with advanced terrain-crossing capabilities.

The purpose of the competitor analysis in this project is not to simply replicate their designs—a strategy that has traditionally guided the NUS RoboMaster team—but to critically evaluate their strengths and limitations. By doing so, the goal is to develop a robot that surpasses existing solutions through better adaptability and innovative mechanical design.

3.2 How Might We Design Framework

The guiding question for this project is framed using the “How Might We” (HMW) design framework:

“How might we engineer a robot platform that can adapt to future RoboMaster terrain challenges, maintain efficient elevation-climbing capability, and incorporate innovative design strategies that maintain competitiveness in game for the Calibur team?”

The HMW framework is adopted because it encourages open and collaborative exploration of design possibilities. “How” reflects the pursuit of a better, future-ready robot; “Might” keeps the process flexible and open to unconventional solutions; and “We” emphasizes collaboration and the integration of insights from existing designs. Together, this approach supports innovative and adaptable problem-solving in developing a terrain-capable RoboMaster chassis.

3.4 Design Statement

Design Statement: “To design a robot that can efficiently climb a 200 mm step within 2 seconds, effectively traverse all current RoboMaster terrain with a speed not less than 1.8 m/s, and incorporate robust, innovative, and adaptive design improvements.”
