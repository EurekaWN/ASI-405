Design Analysis: Comparison of Concept Variants

Existing Design Concepts

Big Wheel

  • Advantage: Big wheel, high terrain-pass capability; fast step climbing
  • Disadvantage: Mechanical complexity; control difficulty; bad gimbal stability
More Info

Performs best on uneven terrain but requires complex balancing and control tuning.

Wheel-Leg / Linkage

  • Advantage: Able to jump, climb steps efficiently; can self recover
  • Disadvantage: Lower terrain-pass capability due to smaller wheel size
More Info

Provides additional degree of freedom for jumping but sacrifices rolling efficiency.

Big Wheel + Self-Balancing

  • Advantage: Big wheel and self-balancing design
  • Disadvantage: Joint motor direct torque; not ideal for weight constraint (30–40 kg)
More Info

Improves stability and adaptability but introduces heavy actuators unsuitable for lightweight robots.
